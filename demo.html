Editor's Note

Volume 18

Welcome back,

The December issue of Yes offers a celebration of women. We look at a variety of great women’s inspiring lives from the last two millennia. There are forgotten scientists and fearless warrior queens, intrepid adventuresses and steadfast1 saints. We also analyse the role of women in literature and art and the factors that have limited their impact. Furthermore, we explore how religion and society have moulded2 the lives of women as well as the crucial role of women in the workplace.

At the same time Yes 18 examines the sexist aspects of English and how gender is reflected in the language. What’s more, we take the opportunity to look at the female-related aspects of pronunciation, false friends, US vs. UK English and idioms. All in all3, Yes 18 offers a thorough4 review of the language of women.

As for the men, in this issue we finally reveal what women want in bed (see p. 51).

This issue is dedicated to all the women who make Your English Supplement possible: Marina, Fabiola, Susannah, Irene T., Marta, Lois, Julie, AmyJo, Saskia, Almudena, Sara, Irene S., Sylvia and all our female readers. The magazine wouldn’t be possible without you.

Over December we will be publishing a series of Christmas blog posts, so keep an eye on5 our webpage (or social media pages).

We all wish you a very Merry Christmas and a prosperous New Year. Remember that there is no issue in January but we will be back with a vengeance6 in February.

Nick Franklin

Editor, Your English Supplement (Yes)
